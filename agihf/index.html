The Framework

The ICC Method™

Three words. One method. Every trade you will ever learn here lives inside this framework.

I
Indication

The 1M candle that closes past the pre-indication level. Not a wick — a close. Price is proving where it wants to go.

C
Correction

Price pulls back to sweep liquidity. Not a reversal — it's the fuel. Corrections are normal. Patient traders wait here.

C
Continuation

Price continues in direction. Take 1:1 partial. Move SL to break even. Let the runner go to 1:2 or structure.

What is a chart actually showing you?

Now that you know you're trading contracts and the market is just buyers vs sellers — let's talk about what you're looking at when you open a chart. It's not lines. It's a story.

~8 min Interactive 3 quiz questions
Every candle is a battle
Green = buyers winning. Red = sellers winning.
A chart is a visual record of the buyers vs sellers battle playing out over time. Every single candle represents one time period.

Green (bullish) candle: Buyers pushed price higher. They won that period.
Red (bearish) candle: Sellers pushed price lower. They won that period.
The body of the candle shows where price opened and closed — where price actually settled. This is the most important part of any candle.
Wicks — the rejected moves
Wicks show where price tried to go — and failed.
A long wick shows price testing a level and getting pushed back. Wick = rejection. Body = acceptance. Remember this — it's the foundation of every ICC trade.
Close beats wick. Always. A wick through a level means price tested it. A candle close past a level means price committed. This is why ICC works.
