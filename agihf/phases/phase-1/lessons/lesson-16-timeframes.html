Timeframes Change the Lens

A timeframe is the lens you use to view price. The market is the same, but the story can look different depending on whether you are looking at a 4H candle, 1H candle, 15M candle, or 1M candle.

~17 minChart foundation+95 XP
Core idea

Same market. Different lens.

A higher timeframe shows the bigger picture. A lower timeframe shows the smaller details. Beginners get confused when they treat every timeframe like it has the same job.

What you know now

🔍
A timeframe is a lens. The market is the same, but the level of detail changes.
🖼️
Higher timeframes show the bigger picture. They help you avoid getting lost in tiny candles.
🧩
Lower timeframes show detail. They are useful after the bigger picture makes sense.
🧠
Timeframes need roles. When every timeframe has a job, the chart feels less chaotic.
