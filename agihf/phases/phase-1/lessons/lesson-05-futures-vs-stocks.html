Futures vs Stocks
This is where a lot of confusion clears up. Futures and stocks are not the same experience, and once you understand the difference, the way you think about trading starts getting cleaner.
~15 min Comparison lesson +70 XP
Core idea
Stocks are about ownership. Futures are about movement.
That is the cleanest way to separate them.

With stocks, you are buying ownership in a company.
With futures, you are trading a contract based on price movement.

That difference changes how you think about direction, risk, flexibility, and why the Dayli ICC method makes more sense in futures.

Detailed breakdown
Why futures fit the way you trade
This is important because the Dayli ICC method is built around reading price behavior and structure, not getting emotionally attached to an asset.

Futures naturally support that because:

1. You can go long or short cleanly.
2. You are focused on movement, not ownership.
3. You are reading behavior and timing, not hoping a company “does well” someday.

That does not make stocks useless. It just means futures line up better with the way you are being trained to think here.

Correct a misconception
“Stocks and futures are basically the same.”
Not really. They can both involve charts and price, but the mindset is different enough that beginners can confuse themselves if they treat them like one thing.
Better understanding
Both use charts, but stocks are ownership-centered and futures are movement-centered.
Bad understanding
They are basically the same, so I can think about them the exact same way.
