✦ Section 2 · Lesson 10

Orders = How You Enter

You don’t just “enter trades.” You choose how you enter them. This lesson is about feeling the difference between jumping in immediately and letting price come to you.

~16 minExecution lesson+95 XP
Core idea

Market orders move now. Limit orders wait.

A market order enters immediately at the current price. A limit order waits for price to come to a specific level. Since your style is mostly limit-based, this lesson makes patience feel like an execution edge, not hesitation.

Teach → Experience

Market order = right now

Press the market order button and watch what happens. The entry fills exactly where price is trading now. No waiting. No pullback. Just immediate execution.

Filled now at current price

Your edge

Limit order = resting below price first

This is closer to how you teach and trade. The limit sits below current price first. Then price pulls back into your level, tags the order, and only after that continues.

Filled on the retest

Reinforce
Patience does not mean doing nothing. It means choosing your price instead of emotionally accepting whatever price is available right now.
