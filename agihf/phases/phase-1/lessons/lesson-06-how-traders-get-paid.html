✦ Phase 1 · Section 1 · Lesson 6
How Traders Get Paid
We already introduced contracts and instrument values. Now we’re going deeper into how price movement turns into money, and more importantly, how that starts feeling different depending on your size.
~16 min Feeling-based lesson +75 XP
Core idea
Points and ticks are how the market moves. P&L is what that movement means to you.
Points are larger units of movement.
Ticks are smaller pieces inside those points.
P&L is the money attached to that movement once you add your instrument and contract count.

This is the lesson where it stops being just chart language and starts becoming real.
Teach → Experience
What is a point? What is a tick?
Use MNQ as the example first. One full point on MNQ is made up of four smaller steps called ticks. Watch the ticks fill up, then answer the question.

Detailed breakdown
How points become money
This is the formula you need in your bones:

Points moved × point value × contracts = P&L

So if MNQ moves 10 points and MNQ is worth $2 per point, then:
10 × $2 = $20 on 1 contract

If you use 5 contracts, that same move becomes:
10 × $2 × 5 = $100

Same move. Different emotional pressure. That is the part people underestimate.
Reinforce
The market is doing what it’s doing. Your size determines how loud that move feels to you.

A regular move can feel calm, loud, or reckless depending on what you attached to it.
Correct a misconception
“More contracts just means more money.”
That’s incomplete. More contracts means more money and more risk, more emotional noise, and less room to think clearly if you are not ready.
Better understanding
More contracts means more reward potential and more risk. I need size that still lets me think clearly.
Bad understanding
If I want to make money faster, I should just use more contracts even if I’m still learning.

What you know now
📏

💵

📣
