Why do markets exist?
Before charts, candles, and entries — there has to be a reason the market exists in the first place. This lesson makes that click without the boring finance lecture.
~12 min Interactive +50 XP
The simple truth
Markets exist because people need somewhere to exchange value, risk, and opportunity.
One side wants in. One side wants out. One side wants protection. One side wants profit.

No exchange = no market. And no market = no movement. This is why price moves at all.
Micro-teach
A market is just a meeting place for decisions.
Think of the market like a giant live negotiation. Buyers say, “I’ll pay this.” Sellers say, “I want that.” Price moves until both sides meet.

That negotiation is what prints the chart.
🤝
Exchange
Someone wants to buy. Someone wants to sell. That’s the first requirement.
🛡️
Risk transfer
Some traders hedge. Others speculate. Both need the market to do that.
⚡
Opportunity
Wherever there’s movement, there’s opportunity to profit from direction.
Who actually needs the market?
Tap the best answer. This interaction should reinforce understanding, not just test memory.
Only day traders
The market exists so retail traders can scalp charts.
Many different participants
Hedgers, institutions, speculators, funds, and traders all use markets differently.
Reinforce
The market is bigger than one trader. That’s why your job is to observe behavior — not assume price should move because of what you personally want.
Teach
No participation means no price movement.
If nobody wants to buy or sell, price has no reason to move. Movement happens because orders are constantly competing and matching.

Markets are alive because participation is alive.

Why this matters for your trading
If you understand why markets exist, you stop seeing price as random. You start seeing it as a response to participation, imbalance, and decision-making.

That mindset is what prepares you for structure later.
