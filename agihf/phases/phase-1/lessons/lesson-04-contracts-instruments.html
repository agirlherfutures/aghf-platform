Contracts & Instruments
This version goes deeper on purpose. I want you to actually see the differences between contracts, instruments, and the dollar amounts attached so this stops feeling abstract.
~16 min Deeper detail +65 XP
Core idea
The instrument is the market. The contract count is your size inside that market.
That distinction has to be crystal clear.

MNQ, NQ, MGC, ES are instruments. They tell you what market you are trading.

1 contract, 2 contracts, 5 contracts tells you how much exposure you have to that instrument’s move. Same market. Different emotional and dollar weight.

Detailed breakdown
How the amounts actually attach
Here’s the simplest way to think about it:

Instrument = the vehicle
Contract count = how many of that vehicle you are driving

If price moves 10 points on MNQ, one contract gives you $20. If that same 10-point move happens on NQ, one contract gives you $200.

That’s why people blow themselves up. They understand the chart a little, but they do not understand the weight attached to the instrument they picked.
Reinforce
Same chart logic. Very different dollar consequence. This is why size and instrument selection matter just as much as the setup.

What you know now

📄

Dollar amounts attach fast. One point means something very different on MNQ than it does on ES or NQ.

Micros keep you teachable. Smaller exposure gives you room to actually learn before your emotions hijack the lesson.
