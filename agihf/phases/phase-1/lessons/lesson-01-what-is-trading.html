What is trading?
Before you ever take a trade, you need to understand what you’re participating in. No fluff. No random jargon. Just the real thing in a way that clicks.
~12 minHyper interactive+50 XP
What you’re really doing
Trading is buyers and sellers disagreeing on price.
Buyers think price should go higher. Sellers think price should go lower. That disagreement creates movement.

That’s it. Everything else you see on a chart is just that battle being printed in real time.
Quick check
Lock the first concept in
Before we move, make sure this is clear.

What just happened
Price did not move randomly. It moved because one side won.

When you tapped Long, you were saying buyers would stay in control. When you tapped Short, you were saying sellers would take over.

Trading is not magic. It’s a decision about who is in control.

What you’re trading
You are not trading candles. You are trading contracts.
In futures, you are not buying the actual thing itself. You’re entering a contract based on price movement.

That means your money is made from direction + distance moved, not ownership.
Quick check
Profit comes from what?
Mindset shift
Futures vs stocks
In stocks, you own something. In futures, you trade movement.

That’s why futures traders focus on direction, behavior, levels, and timing — not emotional attachment.
Experience
Which one sounds like futures trading?
Money math
Points, ticks, and P&L
Every market moves in measurable pieces. Those pieces convert into money. Before you trade, you should know:

How much one point is worth.
How much one tick is worth.
How much your size changes your risk.
Simple breakdown
MNQ = $2 per point
MGC = $10 per point

The move may be the same, but your contract size changes the money outcome. That’s where discipline starts.
